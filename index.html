01

What is the difference between an HMO and a PPO plan?

  • HMO (Health Maintenance Organization): Requires you to choose a primary care physician (PCP) and get referrals from them to see specialists. Care is typically only covered if you use doctors and hospitals within the HMO network.
  • PPO (Preferred Provider Organization): Offers more flexibility. You don’t need a referral to see a specialist, and you can see out-of-network doctors, though at a higher cost. PPOs generally have higher premiums than HMOs.

02

What is a deductible in a health insurance plan?

  • A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket for healthcare services before your insurance begins to cover costs. For example, if your deductible is $1,000, you’ll need to pay that amount for covered services before your insurance starts contributing. Some services, like preventive care, may be covered before the deductible is met.

03

What is the difference between a premium and a copay?

  • Premium: This is the amount you pay regularly (usually monthly) to keep your health insurance coverage active.
  • Copay: A copay is a fixed amount you pay for specific services (e.g., $30 for a doctor visit) after you've met your deductible. It applies to certain healthcare services like doctor visits, prescriptions, or emergency room visits.

04

What is coinsurance and how does it work?

  • Coinsurance is the percentage of costs you share with your insurance company after you've met your deductible. For example, if your coinsurance is 20%, and you’ve met your deductible, the insurance company will cover 80% of the cost of covered services, and you’ll pay 20%. This applies until you reach your out-of-pocket maximum.

05

What does "out-of-pocket maximum" mean?

  • The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you'll pay in a policy period (typically a year) for covered healthcare services. Once you reach this limit, the insurance company will cover 100% of your covered services for the rest of the policy period. This includes your deductible, copays, and coinsurance but does not include your premiums.
